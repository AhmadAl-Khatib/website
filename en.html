About Ahmad Al Khatib

Ahmad Abdul Qader Al Khatib was born in 1989 in Mazboud village, Chouf District, Lebanon. He is the son of the late engineer Abdelkader Al Khatib and the diligent Samer Malek Al Khatib.

Career: During his university years, Ahmad founded several modest businesses and gained hands-on experience in his family company. He later helped launch multiple enterprises across the Arab world and served as a consultant to prominent regional figures.

Education: Ahmad completed his primary education at Beit Al Hikma School and secondary at Anout Official Secondary School. He holds a Bachelor's in Business Administration from the Arts, Sciences and Technology University in Lebanon, and a Higher Diploma in Software from the UK International College London. He has also completed various global university programs and earned certificates in leadership and management.

Activities

Socio-Political Activities

2015 Waste Crisis

In 2015, we joined national figures and civil society in Lebanon’s protests against government negligence that flooded the streets with garbage. I personally submitted a German-funded proposal to convert waste into electricity at no public expense, but sectarian quotas and entrenched corruption prevented its realization.

17 October 2019 Uprising

We were early, dedicated participants in the nationwide mobilization against corruption. When we saw attempts to hijack the movement’s genuine voice, we founded the 17 October People’s Bloc to preserve its integrity. Dr. Khaled Hanqir chaired it, and I served as Secretary-General.
