About Ahmad Al Khatib

Ahmad Abdul Qader Al Khatib, a humble citizen born in Mazboud village, Chouf District, Lebanon in 1989. My father was the late engineer Abdul Qader Al Khatib, and my mother is the hard-working fighter Samer Malik Al Khatib.

Career: During university studies, I established several modest small businesses. I also worked independently with the family company to gain practical work experience before transitioning to contribute to launching several companies in the Arab world. I have served as a consultant to several prominent figures in the Arab world.

Education: Completed primary education at Beit Al Hikma School, secondary education at Anout Official Secondary School, and university studies at the University of Arts, Sciences and Technology where I obtained a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration. I then earned a Higher Diploma in Software from the UK International College London. I have also participated in courses organized by major global universities and obtained several certificates in leadership and management.

Socio-Political Activities

2015 Waste Crisis

We participated with a group of national figures and civil society in the 2015 Lebanon protests resulting from government negligence that flooded streets with waste. At that time, I personally presented a proposal to the Lebanese government (with German funding) to address the waste crisis and convert it into a source of income by transforming it into electrical energy according to environmental standards without costing the Lebanese state a single penny. However, sectarian quotas and rampant corruption in state institutions prevented the realization of this development project for Lebanon as usual.

17 October 2019

Regardless of differences in terminology between revolution, uprising, or movement, we were among the first participants in these popular national movements protesting against corruption. When we noticed some disruptive attempts to hijack this sincere popular wave and divert it from its path, we established the 17 October Popular Bloc to preserve the civilized face of the movement and confront attempts to divert it from the people's legitimate demands, chaired by Dr. Khaled Hanqir, and I served as Secretary-General of the Bloc.
