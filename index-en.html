About Ahmad Al-Khatib

Ahmad Abdul Qadir Al-Khatib, a humble citizen born in Mazboud village, Al-Shouf region, Lebanon in 1989. My father was the late engineer Abdul Qadir Al-Khatib, and my mother is the hardworking fighter in life, Samar Malik Al-Khatib.

Work: Simultaneously with university studies, I founded several modest small businesses. I also worked (as a freelancer) with the family company to gain practical experience in the job market before transitioning to work and contributing to the launch of several companies in the Arab world. I have also served as a consultant for several well-known figures in the Arab world.

Education: I completed my primary education at Beit Al-Hikma School, secondary education at Anout Public Secondary School, and university studies at the University of Arts, Sciences and Technology where I obtained a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration. I then earned a Higher Diploma in Software from the UK International College London. I have also participated in several courses organized by major international universities and obtained several certificates in leadership and management.

Socio-Political Activities

2015 Waste Crisis

I participated with a group of national figures and civil society in the 2015 Lebanon protests resulting from the negligence of the Lebanese state that flooded the streets with waste. On a personal level, I presented a proposal to the Lebanese state (with German funding) to solve the waste crisis and turn it into a source of income for the treasury by converting it into a source of electrical energy according to environmental standards without costing the Lebanese state a single penny. However, sectarian quotas and rampant corruption in the state pillars prevented the realization of this development project for Lebanon as usual.

October 17, 2019

Regardless of the different names between revolution, uprising, or movement, we were among the first participants in these comprehensive national popular movements protesting against corruption since the beginning of October 17. When we noticed some saboteurs attempting to hijack this sincere popular wave and infiltrate it to divert it from its path, we established the October 17 Popular Bloc to preserve the civilized face of October 17 and confront attempts to divert it from the legitimate demands of the people, headed by Dr. Khaled Hanqir, and I served as the General Secretariat representative for the bloc.

My recent book: Learn Python in Less Than a Week

Book Summary: Do you want to double your income by learning an enjoyable and effortless skill? Do you want to change and simplify your life? Do you want to be part of the digital technology revolution that is changing the face of our contemporary world? If your answer is yes, and you've wondered where and how to start, I strongly believe this book is what you're looking for. Learn the profession of the era, which I mean programming, specifically the wonderful language (Python), from scratch to professionalism.

Whether you're a complete beginner far from this field or a professional programmer planning to learn Python, this comprehensive book covers most Python basics for you. Get to know the concepts of this wonderful language in a fun and easy way, and be able to apply what you learn in creating various practical and useful programs. After reading it, you'll become a skilled programmer capable of keeping up with modern developments in the world of computing.

The book is formulated simply and smoothly for everyone to understand, presented in an almost interactive style full of explanations so you can learn it on your own without needing additional lessons or training courses.
